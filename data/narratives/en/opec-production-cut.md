## Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis examines the OPEC production cut scenario across the Gulf Cooperation Council member states. The scenario models a coordinated 5-8% reduction in crude oil production implemented over a 12-month horizon, representing a critical geopolitical and economic shock with cascading implications for regional stability, fiscal sustainability, and strategic positioning.

### Key Findings

- **Aggregate Economic Impact**: GDP contraction of 2.3-3.8% across the GCC region
- **Fiscal Vulnerability**: High oil-dependent economies face immediate budget pressures
- **Geopolitical Dimension**: Production cuts reflect OPEC consensus with implications for Saudi leadership
- **Strategic Response Window**: 12-month horizon allows for contingency planning and policy adjustment
- **Sectoral Variation**: Non-oil sectors experience differential impact based on fiscal stimulus capacity
